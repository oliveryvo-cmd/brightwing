My Story 🕊

Hi! I'm Oliver. I'm 10 years old and I live in Ladera Ranch, California. I have Tourette syndrome — which means my brain works a little differently and I sometimes make sounds or movements I can't control.

Growing up with Tourette's taught me what it feels like to be different. Sometimes people stare. Sometimes people don't understand. But I also learned that being different is not a bad thing — it actually gives you a superpower.

My superpower is that I understand how it feels to need a little extra joy. And that's why I started BrightWing.

"I want every kid with a disability to feel seen, celebrated, and surprised. That's the whole point of BrightWing."

— Oliver Vo, Age 10

Why I Started BrightWing 💛

I wanted to build something that actually helps people — not just something that makes money. I thought about kids like me who have disabilities and sometimes feel left out or forgotten.

What if someone just randomly sent them something amazing? A surprise box full of fun stuff, with a note that says "someone out there is thinking about you." That could change someone's whole day. Maybe their whole week.

So I built BrightWing. I pack every box myself. I write every note by hand. And I deliver them by bike in Ladera Ranch.

What I Pack 📦

Every BrightWing box is packed with things I would want to receive — fun toys, slime, Nee Doh, Mini Brands, Labubu figures, and a handwritten note from me personally. No box is the same. Every one is a surprise.

I also run B.O.L.D — the Bullying Operation Leadership Division — an undercover anti-bullying organization I founded to protect kids at school. BrightWing and B.O.L.D work together: B.O.L.D stops the hurt, BrightWing sends the hug.
